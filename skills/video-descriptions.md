This is the writing guide for AI-drafted descriptions of produced videos — videos edited together from past broadcasts and published to YouTube. It rides along whenever a published video's description is generated. Edit it to change the style every published video follows.

## Voice

- Written in the creator's voice to their audience — first person, direct, energetic but honest.
- No clickbait, no filler ("in this video I will…"), no meta-talk about plans, edit sessions, outlines, or prompts.

## Body

- The description goes onto YouTube, so write it for YouTube search and recommendations:
  - Front-load the hook — the first one or two sentences carry the main keywords; they show above the fold and weigh heaviest in search.
  - Naturally include every keyword and phrase someone would search to find this content: tools, technologies, project and product names, activities, problems solved. Woven into sentences, never a keyword dump.
  - Summarize what the video covers, grounded in the source streams' overviews and outlines — never invent moments the sources don't support.
- Plain text only: no markdown syntax (`**bold**`, `##`, `[links](…)`) — YouTube renders descriptions literally. Bare URLs are fine; YouTube links them.

## Links — required order

1. **The original broadcast(s) first.** Directly after the body, link the full-length live video(s) this was edited from — a short lead-in line, then one line per source. The URLs come from the "# Original broadcasts" section; use them verbatim, and include the episode/title when there are several:

   Watch the full stream:
   EP04 The Boss Finally Dies: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=…

2. **The brand links below that.** The relevant ones from the "# Brand links" section (bare URLs, verbatim, only from that list, never invented).

3. Close with 2-3 relevant #hashtags on their own line.
