This is the creative brief for generated project cover images. When a cover is generated from a project's Overview page, this document is sent to the image model together with the project's name and description, the brand's assets (see below) — and, when changes are requested, the current image plus the feedback. Edit it to change the style every project cover follows.

## What a project cover is

- A project cover is closer to a **logo or title card** than a stream thumbnail: it identifies a body of work — a launch, a build, a campaign — that the producer returns to over weeks or months.
- The subject is the **project itself**, not an episode or a moment: lead with the project's name, supported by a short tagline when the description offers one.
- It should look at home on a project card, a docs page, or a pitch deck — calm, deliberate, and brandable rather than loud.

## Brand

- The brand's image assets ride along as references, and every asset is listed with its description under "Brand assets" in the request. Follow those descriptions: place the logo where its description asks, match the palette exactly, and keep any mascot or recurring character consistent with its art.
- Brand identity outranks the generic style rules below — where they conflict, the brand wins.
- With no brand assets provided, the style rules below stand alone.

## Composition

- Landscape 16:9, designed to read clearly at card size in the Projects grid.
- The project **name is the focal point** — a strong, well-set wordmark treatment, centered or confidently off-center.
- A single supporting motif drawn from what the project actually is (its domain, tooling, or subject per the description) — abstract or iconographic, behind or beside the name, never competing with it.
- Flat or gently textured backgrounds; restrained palette (two or three colors). No busy scenes.

## Text

- The project name, rendered large and legible — this is the one place the full name belongs.
- Optionally **one short tagline** (3–7 words) distilled from the description: what the project is or promises. Skip it if the description offers nothing crisp.
- Clean, modern lettering; no more than these two text elements.

## Hard rules

- No streaming dressing of any kind: no LIVE badges, viewer counts, platform logos, player chrome, or countdown framing.
- No real people's likenesses.
- No walls of text, no fine print, nothing that vanishes at card size.
