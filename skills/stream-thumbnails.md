This is the creative brief for generated stream thumbnails. When a thumbnail is generated from the Plan Stream form, this document is sent to the image model together with the stream's title and description, the brand's assets (see below) — and, when changes are requested, the current image plus the feedback. Edit it to change the style every thumbnail follows.

## Brand

- The brand's image assets ride along as references, and every asset is listed with its description under "Brand assets" in the request. Follow those descriptions: place the logo where its description asks, match the palette exactly, and keep any mascot or recurring character consistent with its art.
- Brand identity outranks the generic style rules below — where they conflict, the brand wins.
- With no brand assets provided, the style rules below stand alone.

## Composition

- Landscape 16:9 thumbnail for a live coding / creator broadcast, designed to read clearly at small sizes (video grids, mobile).
- One strong focal subject placed off-center, with generous negative space for text on the opposite side.
- Bold, saturated colors with high contrast against both light and dark backgrounds; a subtle vignette is fine, busy backgrounds are not.

## Text

- At most one short phrase (3-5 words) distilled from the stream title — never the full title verbatim if it is long.
- Big, thick, sans-serif lettering with strong contrast (outline or backing shape if needed). No small text anywhere.
- Episode numbers only when they are the hook.

## Content

- Reflect the actual topic from the title and description — tools, code, hardware, or the project being built — rather than generic "tech" imagery.
- Energetic but honest: no clickbait arrows, no shocked faces, no red circles.

## Hard rules

- No platform logos or watermarks, no fake player chrome (progress bars, play buttons).
- No real people's likenesses; a stylized/illustrated streamer figure is fine.
- No walls of text, no more than two text elements total.
