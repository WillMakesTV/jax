Editing preferences for **long-form** videos (8–15 minutes, horizontal). These are the producer's standing corrections — the things they have had to ask for, pass after pass, on videos the editor got almost right. Follow them on the first cut so they don't have to be asked for again.

This document is written by the producer, and grown by the app: when they finish editing a long-form video and press "Update the long-form video skill with these changes", the edits they requested on that video are folded in here. It should read as a set of rules, not as a diary of one video.

## Standing rules

- **Earn the runtime.** Pick the length the material actually supports. A tight 8 minutes beats a slack 15; never pad to reach a number.
- **Cold open.** Lead with the strongest moment in the video, then the title card, then the setup.
- **Cut the dead ground.** Silence, tangents, failed attempts that go nowhere, and the long middle of anything repetitive. Keep the reactions, the punchlines, and the moments the outline calls out.
- **Let the good moments breathe.** Tight is not the same as rushed — a landed joke or a hard-won win needs a beat after it.
- **Signpost the structure.** Title cards or chapter beats between segments, so a viewer always knows where they are.
